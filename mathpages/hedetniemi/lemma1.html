(lemma 1) Given graphs \(G,H,K\), we have \(G \times H \to K \iff H \to K^G\)
Let \( \phi: G \times H \to K \) be an homomorphism and we construct an homomorphism \(\gamma : H \to K^G\) as follow: \[ \gamma(h) = \gamma_h, \gamma_h(a)= \phi((a,h)) \] \(\gamma\) is indeed an homomorphism since:
\((h_1,h_2) \in E(H), (g_1,g_2) \in E(G) \Rightarrow (\gamma_{h_1}(g_1),\gamma_{h_2}(g_2)) =(\phi((g_1,h_1)), \phi((g_2,h_2))) \in E(K)\)
Therefore, \( H \to K^G\).
Conversely, if we are given homomorphism \(\gamma : H \to K^G\), we can construct homomorphism \(\phi: G \times H \to K\) as: \[ \phi((a,h))=\gamma(h)(a)\] \(\phi\) is also an homomorphism because if we have \(((a_1,h_1),(a_2,h_2)) \in E(G \times H) \), then
\( (\phi((a_1,h_1)), \phi((a_2,h_2)))=(\gamma(h_1)(a_1),\gamma(h_2)(a_2))\).
Since \(\gamma\) is an homomorphism, \( (\gamma(h_1),\gamma(h_2)) \in E(K^G) \).
Together with the fact that \((a_1,a_2) \in G\) we get \( (\gamma(h_1)(a_1),\gamma(h_2)(a_2)) \in E(K)\)
