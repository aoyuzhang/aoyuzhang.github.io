(lemma 5) Given graph \(G\) and \(c,q \in \mathbb{N} \), a suited proper coloring \( \Lambda : \mathcal{E}_c(G \boxtimes K_q) \to [c] \), restriction of \(\Lambda\) to the mappings that are constant on the cliques \( \{g\} \times K_q \subset G \boxtimes K_q \) is a proper colorings \( \psi : \mathcal{E}_c(\Gamma_G) \to [c] \) where we indentify clique \(\{g\} \times K_q\) to \(g\).
We associate the map \(f \in \mathcal{E}_c(\Gamma_G)\) with the map \(g \in \mathcal{E}_c(G \boxtimes K_q) \) if \(g\) is constant on all cliques \( \{m\} \times K_q \subset (G \boxtimes K_q) \) and \(f(m) = g((m,d)) , d \in V(K_q), \forall m \in V(G)\). We write \(f \sim g\) if we can associate \(f\) with \(g\). Next, we just define \(\psi : \mathcal{E}_c(\Gamma_G) \to [c] \) as \(\psi(f) := \Lambda(g) \) if \(f \sim g\). It remains to show \(\psi\) is a proper coloring of \(\mathcal{E}_c(\Gamma_G)\). So suppose that \( (a,b) \in E(\mathcal{E}_c(\Gamma_G)) \), we want to show \(\psi(a) \neq \psi(b) \). We know that \(\forall (u,v) \in E(\mathcal{E}_c(\Gamma_G)), a(u) \neq b(v) \). And now, let \(k,l\ \in \mathcal{E}_c(G \boxtimes K_q) \) be such that \(a \sim k, b \sim l\). It suffices to show \(\Lambda(k) \neq \Lambda(l)\). There are three types of edges in \(\mathcal{E}_c(G \boxtimes K_q)\), we have \[ \begin{gather*} ((u_1,v_1),(u_1,v_2)), u_1 \in V(G)\\ ((u_1,v_1),(u_2,v_2)), (u_1,u_2) \in E(G)\\ ((u_1,v_1),(u_2,v_1))) \end{gather*} \] For the first type, we have \(k((u_1,v_1))=a(u_1) \neq b(u_1) = l((u_1,v_2))\). Here we used the fact that \((u_1,u_1) \in E(\Gamma_G)\). For the second type, we hace \(k((u_1,v_1))=a(u_1) \neq b(u_2) = l((u_2,v_2))\). This is clear as \((u_1,u_2) \in E(\Gamma_G)\). For the third type, we have \(k((u_1,v_1))=a(u_1) \neq b(u_2) = l((u_2,v_1))\). This is clear as \((u_1,u_2) \in E(\Gamma_G)\).
Hence, \( \Lambda(k) \neq \Lambda(l) \) and this implies \( \psi(a) \neq \psi(b)\) and this implies \(\psi\) is a proper coloring.
