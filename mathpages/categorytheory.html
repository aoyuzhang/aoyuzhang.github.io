    Ok fine let's start with the definition of Category:
    A \(\textbf{category}\) \(\mathscr{A}\) consists of:
    • a collection \(ob(\mathscr{A})\) of objects
    • for two object \(A,B \in ob(\mathscr{A})\), a collection \(\mathscr{A}(A,B)\) of maps called morphisms from \(A\) to \(B\),
    • for each \(A, B, C \in ob(\mathscr{A})\), a function \[ \mathscr{A}(B,C) \times \mathscr{A}(A,B) \to \mathscr{A}(A,C) \] that sends \((g,f) \to g \circ f\), that is called \(\textbf{composition}\).
    • For each \(A \in ob(\mathscr{A}) \), there is an element \(1_A\) of \(\mathscr{A}(A,A)\) that is called identity on \(A\)
    that satisfies:
    • for each \(f \in \mathscr{A}(A,B), g \in \mathscr{A}(B,C), h \in \mathscr{A}(C,D)\) we have \((h \circ g) \circ f = h \circ (g \circ f) \)
    • for each \(f \in \mathscr{A}(A,B) \), we have \(f \circ 1_A = f = 1_B \circ f \).
    If \( f \in \mathscr{A}(A,B)\) then we call \(A\) \(\textbf{domain}\) of \(f\) and \(B\) the \(\textbf{codomain}\) of \(f\).
    A example of category is the category of group \(\textbf{Grp}\) with morphisms the group homomorphisms.
    A map \(f : A \to B\) in a category \(\mathscr{A}\) is an \(\textbf{isomorphism}\) if there exists a map \(g: B \to A \) such that \(gf =1_A\) and \(fg = 1_B\). we call \(g\) the \(\textbf{inverse}\) of \(f\) and write \(g = f^{-1}\). If there is isomorphism from \(A\) to \(B\), we say \(A, B\) are \(\textbf{isomorphic}\).
    For example, in the category of group, the isomorphism between two object is exactly the group isomorphism.
    A \(\textbf{discrete}\) category is a category consisting of objects with their identity maps and no other morphism between objects.
    Every category \(\mathscr{A}\) has an \(\textbf{opposite}\) or \(\textbf{dual}\) category that is denoted by \(\mathscr{A}^{op}\), that is defined by reversing the arrow representing morphism between objects.
    Given categories \(\mathscr{A}, \mathscr{B}\), the \(\textbf{product category} \) is denoted by \(\mathscr{A} \times \mathscr{B}\) and is defined as:
    • \(ob(\mathscr{A} \times \mathscr{B}) = ob(\mathscr{A}) \times ob(\mathscr{B}) \)
    • \((\mathscr{A} \times \mathscr{B})((A,B),(A',B')) = \mathscr{A}(A,A') \times \mathscr{B}(B,B')\)
    Let \(\mathscr{A}, \mathscr{B}\) be categories. A \(\textbf{functor}\) \(F : \mathscr{A} \to \mathscr{B}\) consists of
    • a function : \(ob(\mathscr{A}) \to ob(\mathscr{B}) \) written as \(A \to F(A)\)
    • for each \(A,A' \in \mathscr{A}, a function \(\mathscr{A}(A,A') \to \mathscr{B}(F(A),F(A')) written as \(f \to F(f)\) that satisfy:
      • \(F(f' \circ f) = F(f') \circ F(f) \) whenever \(f\) is an arrwo from \(A\) to \(A'\) and \(f'\) is an arrow from \(A'\) to \(A''\).
      • F(1_A)=1_{F(A)}
